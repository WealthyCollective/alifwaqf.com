What is a Waqf?

Islam's most powerful
tool for lasting legacy

A Waqf (وقف) is an Islamic endowment — an asset permanently ring-fenced for the benefit of designated causes. The principal can never be spent. Only the income flows out, indefinitely, to serve the ummah.

This is not a donation. A Waqf is a permanent act of worship — Sadaqah Jariyah — continuing charity that earns reward long after you are gone.

The Alif Venture Capital Fund is anchored by Yaqeen Institute and Aman Foundation — two of the most trusted Islamic institutions on earth. Their Waqf capital anchors first close. Returns flow back into their permanent endowments. The fund operates under a Wakala + Ju'alah structure — Shariah-clean, LP-familiar, and fatwa-backed by Sheikh Dr. Faid Mohammed Said.

01

02
Only Returns Are Distributed
The investment returns — not the principal — flow to education, health and community causes globally. The capital stays working. The causes keep benefiting.

FAQ

Your questions, answered.

Where does my money actually go?+
It depends on the path you choose.

If you donate to the Waqf: Your transfer goes directly to a registered charity — either Yaqeen Institute (US 501(c)(3)) or Aman Foundation (UK registered charity) — both of which hold and govern the Alif Waqf endowment. No intermediaries. Tax-deductible in the US and UK respectively. The principal is permanently deployed and can never be returned or withdrawn.

If you invest as an LP: Your capital goes to the Alif Venture Capital Fund investment management company, which deploys into global startups, growth equity, real assets and FoF. On exits, 25% of all carried interest flows permanently back to the Waqf. You receive your 65% pro-rata LP returns. The team receives 10%.
How does the Waqf operate its fund mechanics?+
The Alif Venture Capital Fund is managed by a Wakala management company — the Shariah-compliant equivalent of a conventional GP. A 2% management fee plus 3% studio fee (5% total) covers operations and in-house portfolio support. Carry is split: 25% flows permanently into the Waqf on every exit, 65% is returned to investors, and 10% goes to the active team. A 5-seat IC — with 2 permanent Waqf seats — governs every investment decision. Luxembourg-domiciled.
Can I ever get my contribution back?+
It depends on your path.

If you donate to the Waqf: No — and that is the point. A Waqf is a permanent, irrevocable endowment under Islamic law. Once you contribute, your principal can never be returned or dissolved — it stays permanently invested, generating returns that flow to causes indefinitely. This is Sadaqah Jariyah at its most powerful.

If you invest as an LP in the fund: Yes — you receive your capital back plus returns on exit. 65% of all carry is returned to you, 25% flows permanently to the Waqf, and 10% goes to the active team. Your principal is at risk as with any fund investment — returns are never guaranteed. The fund management team's track record indicates 5× returns or greater historically. Luxembourg-domiciled. Professionally managed.

Who governs the Waqf?+
The Alif Waqf is governed by an independent board of trustees under the Shariah supervision of Sheikh Dr. Faid Mohammed Said — Secretary-General of the Commission of Fatwa & Sharia Affairs. Founding institutional partners are the Aman Foundation (Pakistan) and Yaqeen Institute (USA).
How is this connected to Alif Venture Capital Fund?+
The Waqf is the anchor LP of the fund — committing $10M at inception, targeting 30% of total Waqf AUM over time. Returns flow back into the permanent endowment. The fund is Luxembourg-domiciled (SICAV/RAIF structure) operating under a Wakala + Ju'alah framework — Shariah-clean and LP-familiar. Two investment paths: via the Waqf (100% of returns to endowment) or as a traditional LP (25% carry to Waqf, 65% to investor). The more the fund grows, the larger the Waqf grows.
What causes does the Waqf support?+
Current designated causes: Islamic education and scholarship (through Yaqeen Institute), Muslim community health infrastructure (through Aman Foundation), and Muslim entrepreneur and youth development.
